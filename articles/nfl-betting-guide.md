## NFL Betting Basics

### How NFL Betting Works

NFL betting centers around three main bet types: point spreads, totals, and moneylines. Understanding these fundamentals is essential before placing any wagers.

**The NFL Betting Week:**
- Lines release Sunday night/Monday morning for next week
- Lines move throughout the week based on betting action
- Sharp money typically comes early; public money comes late
- Most bets placed Sunday morning before kickoff

### NFL Point Spread Betting

The point spread is the most popular NFL bet type. It levels the playing field between favorites and underdogs.

**How Spreads Work:**

| Team | Spread | Odds |
|------|--------|------|
| Kansas City Chiefs | -6.5 | -110 |
| Denver Broncos | +6.5 | -110 |

**Chiefs -6.5:** Chiefs must win by 7 or more points to cover
**Broncos +6.5:** Broncos must lose by 6 or fewer (or win) to cover

### NFL Total (Over/Under) Betting

Totals bet on the combined score of both teams.

**Example:**
- Chiefs vs. Broncos Total: 47.5
- Over 47.5 (-110): Combined score 48+ wins
- Under 47.5 (-110): Combined score 47 or fewer wins

**Factors Affecting NFL Totals:**
- Weather (wind, rain, cold)
- Pace of play
- Offensive/defensive matchups
- Injury status of key players
- Indoor vs. outdoor venues
- Game script expectations

**Totals Strategy:**
- Weather has significant impact (10+ mph wind depresses scoring)
- Division games often go under (familiarity breeds defense)
- Track coaching tendencies (aggressive vs. conservative)
- Prime-time games historically go under (more defensive game planning)

### NFL Moneyline Betting

Moneyline bets simply pick the winner. No spread involved.

**Example:**
| Team | Moneyline |
|------|-----------|
| Chiefs | -280 |
| Broncos | +230 |

**Chiefs -280:** Bet $280 to win $100 (total return $380)
**Broncos +230:** Bet $100 to win $230 (total return $330)

**When to Bet Moneyline:**
- When you like a favorite but not by the spread
- Underdogs in tight games (spread might be -1 or -2)
- Parlays (moneylines are cleaner for multiple legs)

## NFL Betting Strategies

### Strategy 1: Home Underdog Betting

NFL home underdogs have historically covered spreads at profitable rates.

**The Data:**
- Home underdogs cover approximately 53-54% against the spread
- Home dogs of +3 to +7 are particularly profitable
- Division game home dogs perform well (familiarity)

**Why It Works:**
- Home field advantage worth approximately 2.5 points
- Public overvalues "better" teams
- Sportsbooks shade lines toward popular teams

**Implementation:**
1. Identify home underdogs weekly
2. Focus on +3 to +7 range
3. Consider divisional matchups
4. Bet early before public money arrives

### Strategy 2: Teaser Betting

Teasers adjust spreads in your favor in exchange for worse odds.

**Standard 6-Point NFL Teaser:**
- Move each spread 6 points
- Typically -120 for two teams
- All legs must win

**Profitable Teaser Spots:**

| Original Line | Teased Line | Crosses Numbers |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| -7.5 to -8.5 | -1.5 to -2.5 | 7, 6, 4, 3 |
| +1.5 to +2.5 | +7.5 to +8.5 | 3, 4, 6, 7 |

**Teaser Math:**
At -120 odds, you need to win 52.4% to break even. Properly constructed teasers (crossing 3 and 7) can hit 73%+ per leg.

Two-leg teaser: 73% x 73% = 53.3% > 52.4% (profitable)

**Teaser Rules:**
- Only tease through key numbers 3 and 7
- Stick to 6-point standard teasers
- Two-team teasers only (don't add legs)
- Don't tease totals (no key numbers)

### Strategy 3: Weather Betting

NFL is the most weather-impacted sport for betting purposes.

**Weather Factors:**

| Condition | Impact | Bet Consideration |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Wind 10+ mph | Depresses scoring | Under, run-heavy teams |
| Wind 15+ mph | Significant impact | Strong under lean |
| Rain/Snow | Slows offense | Under, rushing advantage |
| Extreme Cold | Ball harder to catch | Under, defensive lean |
| Dome Teams Outdoors | Adjustment issues | Fade dome teams in weather |

**Weather Betting Process:**
1. Check weather forecasts Thursday-Sunday
2. Identify games with significant weather
3. Analyze which team is better suited
4. Compare your assessment to lines

**Tools:**
- National Weather Service
- NFL weather tracking sites
- Wind specifically impacts passing games

### Strategy 4: Fade Public Favorites

When the public heavily backs a favorite, value often exists on the underdog.

**Identifying Public Sides:**
- Large market teams (Cowboys, Patriots, Chiefs)
- Teams on winning streaks
- Primetime games
- Nationally televised matchups

**The Process:**
1. Track public betting percentages
2. Identify games with 75%+ public on favorite
3. Consider underdog value
4. Combine with other factors (home dog, divisional, weather)

**Why It Works:**
- Sportsbooks shade lines toward public teams
- Creates extra value on unpopular sides
- Casual bettors overvalue recent performance

### Strategy 5: Bye Week Analysis

Teams coming off bye weeks have historical advantages.

**Bye Week Trends:**
- Teams off bye vs. non-bye: approximately 53% ATS
- Worth roughly 1-1.5 points of spread adjustment
- Coaching preparation advantage
- Injury recovery time

**Counter-Consideration:**
- Sportsbooks now account for bye advantage in lines
- Edge has diminished but still exists for certain situations
- Best when combined with other factors

### Strategy 6: Divisional Game Betting

Division games have unique characteristics affecting betting.

**Divisional Trends:**
- Closer games than non-divisional matchups
- Underdogs cover at higher rates
- Familiarity benefits defense
- Often lower scoring

**Strategy:**
- Lean underdogs in divisional matchups
- Consider unders (teams know each other)
- Late-season divisional games are tightest

### Strategy 7: Revenge Game Analysis

The "revenge game" narrative is often overvalued by public bettors.

**Research Shows:**
- Revenge spots show minimal actual performance boost
- Public overvalues narrative
- Creates value fading revenge teams

**Implementation:**
- Identify heavily promoted revenge narratives
- If line seems inflated for revenge team, consider other side
- Don't buy into emotional storylines

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do NFL spreads work?

NFL spreads assign a point handicap to each team. The favorite must win by more than the spread; the underdog must lose by less than the spread (or win outright). For example, Chiefs -6.5 means Kansas City must win by 7+ points to cover.

### What is the best NFL betting strategy?

The best NFL betting strategy combines line shopping across multiple sportsbooks, teaser betting through key numbers, and targeting home underdogs. No single strategy guarantees success, but disciplined execution of proven methods improves long-term results.

### Should I bet NFL spreads or moneylines?

Spreads are typically better value for favorites. Moneylines make sense when you like a favorite to win but not by the full spread, or for small underdogs where the spread is minimal. Experienced bettors use both strategically.

### What are NFL key numbers?

Key numbers are the most common margins of victory. In NFL, 3 (field goal) and 7 (touchdown) are most important. Games land on 3 approximately 15% of the time. Spreads at or near these numbers deserve special attention.

### When should I bet NFL games?

Sharp money typically comes early (Sunday-Tuesday for the following week). Public money comes late (Saturday-Sunday morning). If you have strong opinions, betting early often gets better numbers. Line shopping is valuable regardless of timing.

### How do NFL teasers work?

Teasers adjust point spreads in your favor (usually 6 points) in exchange for reduced odds. A Chiefs -8 becomes Chiefs -2 in a 6-point teaser. All teaser legs must win. When properly constructed through key numbers, teasers can be profitable.

### What percentage of NFL bets should I win?

At standard -110 odds, you need to win 52.4% of bets to break even. Profitable NFL bettors typically win 53-56% long-term. Anything above 55% is excellent. Track your results by bet type to identify strengths.
