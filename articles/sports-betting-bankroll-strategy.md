## Defining Your Bankroll: The Foundation

Your bankroll is the total amount of money dedicated to sports betting. It's separate from your living expenses, savings, and emergency fund.

### How to Set Your Bankroll

**Rule #1: Money You Can Lose**

Your bankroll should be money that, if you lost 100% of it, would not affect your life in any meaningful way. Not your rent money. Not your savings. Not your kid's college fund.

This isn't pessimism; it's risk management. If losing your bankroll would cause genuine hardship, the psychological pressure will sabotage your decision-making.

**Rule #2: Enough to Absorb Variance**

Your bankroll needs to survive losing streaks. Even excellent handicappers face stretches of 10+ consecutive losses. Your bankroll must handle this without going to zero.

**Recommended Starting Bankrolls by Bettor Type:**

| Bettor Type | Recommended Bankroll | Reasoning |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Casual/Recreational | $200-$500 | Enough to bet responsibly without meaningful financial risk |
| Serious Hobbyist | $1,000-$3,000 | Supports proper unit sizing and withstands variance |
| Semi-Professional | $5,000-$20,000 | Enables meaningful returns while managing risk |
| Professional | $20,000+ | Required for betting to generate significant income |

**Rule #3: Never Add to Cover Losses**

If you lose your bankroll, you're done until you've taken a significant break and can afford to set aside new money. Adding to your bankroll to chase losses is the path to financial disaster.

## Understanding Units: The Universal Betting Currency

Professional bettors don't think in dollars; they think in units. Units standardize bet sizing relative to your bankroll and enable comparison across different bankroll sizes.

### What is a Unit?

A unit is a fixed percentage of your bankroll, typically 1-5%.

**Example:**
- Bankroll: $2,000
- Unit size: 2%
- One unit: $40

### Why Units Matter

**Standardization:** When someone says they're up 15 units, you can compare that performance regardless of whether their bankroll is $500 or $50,000.

**Risk Control:** Thinking in units prevents emotionally inflated bet sizes. "I'll bet $500" sounds scarier than "I'll bet 1 unit" even when they're the same thing.

**Performance Tracking:** Measuring results in units shows your true skill independent of bankroll size.

### Choosing Your Unit Size

| Unit Size | Risk Level | Units in Bankroll | Best For |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|----------|
| 1% | Conservative | 100 units | New bettors, risk-averse, large bankrolls |
| 2% | Moderate | 50 units | Most recreational bettors |
| 3% | Aggressive | 33 units | Confident handicappers with verified edge |
| 5% | Very Aggressive | 20 units | Only with significant demonstrated edge |

**Our Recommendation:** Start at 1-2%. You can always increase later, but you can't recover money lost to aggressive early sizing.

## Bankroll Management Systems

Several approaches exist for managing your bankroll. Each has strengths and appropriate use cases.

### Flat Betting: The Foundation

Flat betting means wagering the same amount on every bet, regardless of perceived confidence.

**How It Works:**
1. Set your unit size (e.g., 2% of bankroll)
2. Every bet is exactly one unit
3. Recalculate unit size periodically (weekly or monthly)

**Example:**
- Bankroll: $1,000
- Unit: $20 (2%)
- Win $300 over a month
- New bankroll: $1,300
- New unit: $26 (2% of $1,300)

**Why Flat Betting Works:**

Flat betting removes the most dangerous element of betting: emotional bet sizing. There's no "putting extra on the lock of the week" that inevitably loses. No chasing losses with larger bets. No inflated confidence translating to inflated risk.

**Best For:**
- New bettors (mandatory)
- Recreational bettors
- Anyone who struggles with discipline
- Bettors who haven't verified their edge

### Confidence-Based Betting: Advanced Approach

Confidence-based betting varies bet size based on your conviction level for each pick.

**How It Works:**
1. Create a rating scale (1-5 is common)
2. Assign confidence level to each bet
3. Bet units equal to confidence level

**Example:**
- 5-star play: 5 units
- 3-star play: 3 units
- 1-star play: 1 unit

**The Danger:**

Most bettors overestimate their confidence calibration. They think their 5-star plays are 65% winners when they're actually 54% winners. This miscalibration loses money faster than flat betting at the same overall volume.

**How to Verify Your Calibration:**

Track your picks by confidence level for at least 200 bets. Calculate actual win rate for each tier. If your 5-star plays don't significantly outperform your 3-star plays, you're not calibrated correctly.

| Confidence | Expected Win Rate | Actual Min Win Rate Needed |
|------------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| 5-star (5 units) | Much higher | 58%+ at -110 |
| 3-star (3 units) | Higher | 55%+ at -110 |
| 1-star (1 unit) | Baseline | 52.4%+ at -110 |

**Best For:** Experienced bettors with 500+ bets tracked and verified calibration.

### The Kelly Criterion: Mathematical Optimization

The Kelly Criterion is a mathematically optimal bet sizing formula used by professional bettors and financial traders.

**The Formula:**

Kelly % = (bp - q) / b

Where:
- b = decimal odds - 1
- p = your estimated probability of winning
- q = probability of losing (1 - p)

**Practical Example:**
- Bet: Team A at +150 odds (2.50 decimal)
- Your estimated probability: 45%
- Calculation:
  - b = 2.50 - 1 = 1.50
  - p = 0.45
  - q = 0.55
  - Kelly = (1.50 x 0.45 - 0.55) / 1.50 = 0.125 / 1.50 = 0.0833
- Kelly recommends betting 8.33% of bankroll

**The Problem With Full Kelly:**

Full Kelly is extremely aggressive. The 8.33% recommendation above would devastate your bankroll during a losing streak. Most Kelly users bet "Half Kelly" (4.2%) or "Quarter Kelly" (2.1%) to reduce variance.

**Best For:** Advanced bettors who can accurately estimate probabilities and handle significant variance.

### Fixed-Percentage Adjusting: Practical Middle Ground

This approach maintains flat betting but adjusts unit size more frequently based on bankroll changes.

**How It Works:**
1. Calculate unit size as fixed percentage of current bankroll
2. Recalculate before each betting session
3. Both increases and decreases with bankroll

**Example Over Time:**
- Week 1: $1,000 bankroll, $20 units
- Week 2: $1,150 bankroll, $23 units (up after good week)
- Week 3: $980 bankroll, $19.60 units (down after bad week)
- Week 4: $1,100 bankroll, $22 units (recovery)

**Advantages:**
- Faster compounding during winning periods
- Automatic protection during losing streaks
- Simple to implement

**Best For:** Bettors who bet frequently and want responsive bankroll management.

## Managing Losing Streaks

Every bettor faces losing streaks. Your system must survive them.

### Expected Losing Streaks

Even with a 55% win rate (exceptional), here's what to expect over 1,000 bets:

| Streak Length | Expected Occurrences |
|---------------|---------------------|
| 3+ losses in a row | ~91 times |
| 5+ losses in a row | ~18 times |
| 7+ losses in a row | ~4 times |
| 10+ losses in a row | ~0.3 times (once every ~3,000 bets) |

These aren't bugs; they're features of probability. Your bankroll must handle them.

### How Different Unit Sizes Handle Losing Streaks

**5-Loss Streak Impact:**
- 1% units: 5% bankroll loss
- 2% units: 10% bankroll loss
- 5% units: 25% bankroll loss
- 10% units: 50% bankroll loss

**10-Loss Streak Impact:**
- 1% units: 10% bankroll loss
- 2% units: 20% bankroll loss
- 5% units: 50% bankroll loss
- 10% units: 100% bankroll loss (broke)

This is why conservative unit sizing isn't cowardly; it's mathematically sound.

### Losing Streak Protocols

**Do NOT:**
- Increase bet sizes to recoup losses faster
- Take on more bets to "get even"
- Bet on sports/leagues you don't normally follow
- Force bets when no good opportunities exist
- Borrow money to continue betting

**DO:**
- Stick to your unit size (or reduce it)
- Take breaks if needed mentally
- Review your process for leaks
- Trust the math over the short term
- Remember that variance is temporary

### When to Reduce Bet Sizing

Consider dropping to half-units during:
- Extended losing streaks (10+ losses or 15+ units down)
- Periods of low confidence in your reads
- Major sports calendar changes (new season start, etc.)
- Personal life stress affecting judgment

You can always return to full units when conditions improve.

The magic of proper bankroll management is compounding. As your bankroll grows, your units grow, and your wins become larger in absolute terms.

This is the power of disciplined bankroll management.

### Taking Profits: The Withdrawal Strategy

Your bankroll is meant to generate value you can actually use. Here's how to withdraw responsibly.

**The 50% Rule:**

When your bankroll doubles, withdraw 50% of the profits.

**Example:**
- Starting bankroll: $2,000
- Grown to: $4,000
- Profit: $2,000
- Withdraw: $1,000 (50% of profit)
- New working bankroll: $3,000

This approach provides real-world value from your betting while maintaining growth.

**The Milestone Rule:**

Set specific withdrawal triggers:
- Withdraw $500 at $3,000 bankroll
- Withdraw $1,000 at $5,000 bankroll
- Withdraw $2,500 at $10,000 bankroll

**The Time-Based Rule:**

Withdraw profits quarterly or annually:
- Calculate total profit over period
- Withdraw 25-50% of that profit
- Continue with remaining bankroll

### Rebuilding After Losses

If your bankroll drops significantly:

**Reduce unit size proportionally:**
- $2,000 bankroll at 2% = $40 units
- Drops to $1,200
- New unit: $24 (2% of $1,200)

**Never maintain the same dollar unit with a smaller bankroll.** Betting $40 units on a $1,200 bankroll (3.3%) is riskier than the original $40 on $2,000 (2%).

## Common Bankroll Mistakes

### Mistake #1: No Defined Bankroll

Betting from your regular checking account without a separate, defined betting bankroll is a recipe for disaster. You'll never know if you're actually profitable, and you'll likely bet more than you should.

**Fix:** Open a separate account or use a specific sportsbook balance as your defined bankroll.

### Mistake #2: Bet Sizing Based on Emotion

"I really like this game, so I'll bet more" is how winning streaks turn into losing streaks. Your "feeling" about a game doesn't change the mathematical reality of variance.

**Fix:** Flat betting removes emotional sizing entirely.

### Mistake #3: Chasing Losses

After a losing day, the temptation to bet more to "get even" is powerful. This is how small losses become catastrophic ones.

**Fix:** Strict daily unit limits. If you lose 5 units, you're done for the day regardless of remaining opportunities.

### Mistake #4: Ignoring Variance

Expecting to win every day, week, or even month is mathematically unrealistic. Variance means even excellent bettors face extended losing periods.

**Fix:** Think in terms of 500+ bets, not daily results. Track rolling averages over months, not days.

### Mistake #5: Overconfidence in "Locks"

There are no locks in sports betting. The "guaranteed winner" that leads to a 5-unit bet can lose just like any other bet.

**Fix:** Maximum bet size limits. Never exceed 2-3 units on any single bet regardless of confidence.

### Mistake #6: No Tracking

Without records, you can't evaluate performance, identify leaks, or know if you're actually profitable.

**Fix:** Track every bet from day one. No exceptions.
