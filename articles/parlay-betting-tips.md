## What is a Parlay Bet?

A parlay combines multiple individual bets into one wager. All selections must win for the parlay to pay. In exchange for this higher difficulty, you get significantly enhanced odds.

**Basic Example:**

Instead of making three separate bets:
- Chiefs -3 (-110): $100 to win $91
- Celtics -5 (-110): $100 to win $91
- Yankees ML (-150): $100 to win $67

You combine them into one $100 parlay that pays approximately $600 if all three win.

### How Parlay Odds Work

Each leg multiplies into the next, creating exponentially larger payouts.

**Standard Parlay Payouts (at -110 odds per leg):**

| Legs | Approximate Odds | $100 Bet Payout |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| 2 | +264 | $364 |
| 3 | +596 | $696 |
| 4 | +1,228 | $1,328 |
| 5 | +2,438 | $2,538 |
| 6 | +4,741 | $4,841 |
| 8 | +17,573 | $17,673 |
| 10 | +64,286 | $64,386 |

### The Parlay Trap

Here's the uncomfortable truth: **parlays have higher house edge than straight bets**.

**The Math:**
- Two-leg parlay should pay +300 (25% probability at fair odds)
- Sportsbooks pay approximately +264
- That's about 9% worse than fair odds

This edge compounds with each leg added. A 10-leg parlay might have 20%+ house edge.

**Why Sportsbooks Love Parlays:**
- Higher margin per dollar wagered
- Recreational bettors love them
- Variance means most bettors lose before luck evens out

Understanding this context is essential before developing a parlay strategy.

## Types of Parlay Bets

### Traditional Parlays

Standard multi-game parlays combining different events:
- NFL spread + NBA spread + NHL moneyline
- All bets must be from different games
- Available at all sportsbooks [AFFILIATE LINK]

### Same Game Parlays (SGPs)

Multiple bets from the same game combined:
- Team spread + game total + player prop
- Correlation between legs affects odds
- Often have worse value than traditional parlays

**Popular SGP Example:**
- Chiefs -3.5
- Game over 48.5
- Travis Kelce over 65.5 receiving yards

### Teasers

Adjusted spreads in exchange for lower payouts:
- Move NFL spreads 6-7 points in your favor
- All legs must still win
- Lower odds than standard parlays but higher win probability

**6-Point NFL Teaser Example:**
- Chiefs -7 becomes Chiefs -1
- Bills +3 becomes Bills +9
- Two-team teaser typically pays around -110 to -120

### Pleasers

Opposite of teasers - move spreads against yourself for higher payouts:
- High risk, high reward
- Not recommended for most bettors
- Extreme house edge

### Round Robins

Multiple parlay combinations from the same group of picks:
- 3 picks can create 3 two-leg parlays + 1 three-leg parlay
- More combinations increase coverage
- Requires larger total wager

### When Parlays Can Make Sense

Despite the math, certain parlay situations can be reasonable:

**1. Correlated Parlays (Where Allowed)**
Some outcomes are connected, making parlays more valuable:
- Team wins AND under total (defensive game scripts)
- Running back TD AND team wins (high correlation)
- Quarterback yards AND team spread

Note: Sportsbooks now limit many correlated parlays or adjust odds significantly.

**2. Positive EV Legs**
If individual legs have positive expected value, parlays compound that edge:
- 3% edge per leg
- Three-leg parlay: approximately 9% total edge
- The math works in your favor

**3. Small Stakes Entertainment**
$5-10 parlays for entertainment value are reasonable if you:
- Understand you're paying for entertainment
- Keep it within your entertainment budget
- Don't expect to profit long-term

## Smart Parlay Betting Strategies

### Strategy 1: Correlated SGPs

Build same-game parlays with logically connected outcomes.

**High Correlation Examples:**

| If This Happens... | This Becomes More Likely... |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| Team wins big | Star player has good stats |
| Game goes to OT | Total goes over |
| Blowout occurs | Total goes under (garbage time) |
| RB dominates | Team covers spread |

**SGP Construction:**
1. Identify a game narrative (blowout, shootout, defensive battle)
2. Select legs that all align with that narrative
3. Avoid contradictory legs

**Example - "Chiefs Blowout" SGP:**
- Chiefs -10.5 alt spread
- Chiefs over 28.5 team total
- Patrick Mahomes over 2.5 passing TDs
- Game under 52.5 total (defensive second half)

All these outcomes become more likely if Chiefs dominate.

### Strategy 2: Small Parlay Sizes

Limit parlays to 2-3 legs maximum.

**Why Smaller is Better:**
- House edge compounds with each leg
- Win probability remains reasonable
- Variance is more manageable

**Two-Leg Parlay Math:**
- 50% x 50% = 25% win probability
- Pays approximately +264 (should be +300)
- Manageable 9% house edge

**Five-Leg Parlay Math:**
- 50% x 50% x 50% x 50% x 50% = 3.125% win probability
- House edge approaches 20%+
- Unsustainable for long-term betting

### Strategy 3: Value Leg Identification

Only parlay legs where you have genuine edge.

**Process:**
1. Handicap each game independently
2. Identify bets with positive expected value
3. Only include +EV legs in parlays
4. Skip games without clear edge

**Example:**
You analyze 10 NFL games and find value on 3 spreads. Parlay only those 3, not random additions to "sweeten the pot."

### Strategy 4: Teasers Over Parlays (Football)

For NFL betting, teasers often provide better value than standard parlays.

**Why Teasers Work:**
- Moving through key numbers (3, 7) significantly increases win probability
- Standard 6-point teasers hit approximately 73% per leg (if crossing 3 and 7)
- Two-team teasers at -120 can be +EV

**Ideal Teaser Spots:**
| Original Line | Teased Line | Key Numbers Crossed |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------|
| -7.5 to -8.5 | -1.5 to -2.5 | 7, 3 |
| +1.5 to +2.5 | +7.5 to +8.5 | 3, 7 |

**Teaser Rules:**
- Only tease through key numbers
- Stick to 2-leg teasers
- Avoid teasers in basketball (no key numbers)

### Strategy 5: Long-Shot Parlays as Lottery Tickets

Treat large parlays as pure entertainment with minimal stakes.

**Lottery Ticket Approach:**
- Maximum 0.5% of bankroll per week on long-shot parlays
- Expect to lose this money
- If one hits, it's a bonus
- Never chase lottery losses with more lottery tickets

**Example Budget:**
- $5,000 bankroll
- $25/week max on 5+ leg parlays
- $500/year entertainment budget for long shots

## Parlay Betting Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Adding Legs for Better Odds

**The Problem:**
"I'll add the Lakers just to boost the payout" - said by every losing parlay bettor.

Adding legs without conviction dilutes your edge and reduces win probability.

**The Fix:**
Only include bets you would make individually. Every leg should stand on its own merit.

### Mistake 2: Chasing with Bigger Parlays

**The Problem:**
After losses, betting bigger parlays to "win it back quickly."

**The Fix:**
Parlay losses are expected. Stick to your bankroll plan and unit sizes. See our [bankroll management guide](/articles/bankroll-management-guide).

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Correlation Penalties

**The Problem:**
SGPs with obvious correlations pay worse than expected because sportsbooks adjust for correlated outcomes.

**The Fix:**
Compare SGP odds to individual leg multiplication. If the SGP pays significantly less, the correlation penalty is too high.

### Mistake 4: Heavy Favorite Parlays

**The Problem:**
Parlaying multiple -300 favorites seems "safe" but offers terrible value.

**Example:**
- 4 legs at -300 each
- Win probability: 81% x 81% x 81% x 81% = 43%
- Typical payout: +135
- You're risking a lot to win a little with high loss probability

**The Fix:**
If you want to parlay favorites, mix in underdogs or avoid parlays altogether.

### Mistake 5: Not Shopping Parlay Lines

**The Problem:**
Different sportsbooks offer different parlay payouts and individual leg lines.

**The Fix:**
Compare parlay odds across multiple books [AFFILIATE LINK]. A 5% difference in parlay payout is significant.

## Calculating Parlay Payouts

### Basic Parlay Math

**For American Odds:**

1. Convert each leg to decimal odds:
   - Positive: (odds/100) + 1
   - Negative: (100/|odds|) + 1

2. Multiply all decimal odds together

3. Convert back to American if needed

**Example:**
- Leg 1: -110 = 1.909
- Leg 2: +150 = 2.500
- Leg 3: -130 = 1.769

Parlay decimal odds: 1.909 x 2.500 x 1.769 = 8.44
Parlay American odds: +744

**$100 Bet Payout:**
$100 x 8.44 = $844 total return ($744 profit)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a parlay bet?

A parlay combines multiple individual bets into one wager. All selections must win for the parlay to pay. The advantage is significantly higher potential payouts. The disadvantage is that losing just one leg loses the entire bet.

### Are parlay bets worth it?

Parlays have higher house edge than straight bets, making them -EV for most bettors. However, they can be worth it for entertainment (small stakes), compounding edges (if you have +EV legs), or exploiting correlations. For serious bettors focused on profit, straight bets are generally better.

### What is the best parlay strategy?

The best parlay strategy is to: keep parlays small (2-3 legs), only include bets where you have genuine edge, exploit correlated outcomes when possible, use teasers instead of parlays for NFL betting, and set strict bankroll limits. Never add legs just to boost payouts.

### How many legs should a parlay have?

For profit-focused betting, limit parlays to 2-3 legs. Each additional leg increases house edge and decreases win probability. The math strongly favors smaller parlays. Large parlays (5+ legs) should only be small-stake entertainment bets.

### What is a same game parlay?

A same game parlay (SGP) combines multiple bets from a single game. For example: team spread + game total + player prop. SGPs allow correlated bets but sportsbooks adjust odds to account for correlation, often providing worse value than traditional parlays.

### What happens if one leg of my parlay pushes?

If one leg pushes (ties), most sportsbooks remove that leg and recalculate the parlay with remaining legs. A 4-leg parlay with one push becomes a 3-leg parlay. This is standard at major sportsbooks [AFFILIATE LINK].

### Should I bet parlays or straight bets?

Straight bets are mathematically superior for long-term profitability due to lower house edge. Parlays should complement straight betting, not replace it. Use parlays strategically (correlations, compounding edge) or for entertainment only.
