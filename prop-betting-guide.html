What Are Prop Bets?

Proposition bets (props) are wagers on specific outcomes within a game that don't directly relate to the final score. They can be about individual player performance, team statistics, or game events.

Types of Prop Bets

Player Props

Bets on individual player performance: points scored, yards gained, strikeouts thrown, etc.

Examples: LeBron James Over 27.5 Points, Patrick Mahomes Over 285.5 Passing Yards

Game Props

Bets on team or game statistics: total touchdowns, first team to score, will there be overtime, etc.

Examples: Total Touchdowns Over 5.5, First Team to Score - Chiefs, Both Teams to Score Over 30

Exotic Props

Fun/novelty bets often available for big events: Super Bowl coin toss, national anthem length, Gatorade color.

Note: These are entertainment bets. The edge is usually with the house.

Player Prop Strategy

Player props are where you can find the most consistent value. Here's why:

  • Less efficient markets: Sportsbooks focus more on main lines. Props often have softer numbers.
  • Information edge: You can research specific matchups better than the books.
  • Correlation opportunities: Combine props that move together (Same Game Parlays).

The Matchup Analysis Method

Don't just look at a player's season averages. Analyze their specific matchup:

  • Defensive rankings: How does the opponent defend this player's position?
  • Pace of play: Fast games = more opportunities = higher stats
  • Recent usage: Is the player's role expanding or shrinking?
  • Injury impacts: Are other players out, creating more opportunities?

Line Shopping for Props

Prop lines vary significantly between books. Always compare:

Sportsbook Player Points Line Odds
DraftKings Over 24.5 -110
FanDuel Over 25.5 -105
BetMGM Over 24.5 -115
Caesars Over 25.5 +100

That full point difference can be the margin between winning and losing. Always shop props.

The "Alternate Lines" Edge

Many books offer alternate prop lines at different odds. If you like Over 24.5 points at -110, check if Over 22.5 at -150 or Over 27.5 at +130 offers better expected value for your confidence level.

Game Prop Strategy

Game props focus on team-level or game-level outcomes. These require different analysis than player props.

Most Profitable Game Props

First Half / First Quarter Lines

These lines are often less sharp than full-game lines. If you have an edge on how a team starts games (fast starters vs. slow starters), exploit it here.

Key data to track: Team's first half vs. second half scoring splits, starting pitcher stats for first 3 innings, etc.

Team Totals

Instead of betting the overall game total, bet on a specific team's score. This isolates your analysis to one side.

Example: You think the Chiefs will score a lot but are unsure about the opponent. Bet Chiefs Team Total Over instead of Game Total Over.

Scoring Props

First to score, last to score, first scoring method (TD, FG, safety). These require understanding of team tendencies:

  • Teams that win the coin toss and receive
  • Teams with strong opening drives
  • Aggressive vs. conservative coaches

How to Research Props

Essential Data Points

  1. Recent form (last 5-10 games): More predictive than season averages
  2. Minutes/usage trends: Is playing time increasing or decreasing?
  3. Home vs. away splits: Many players perform differently by location
  4. Opponent-specific stats: How do they perform against this team/defense?
  5. Rest/schedule: Back-to-backs, travel, etc.

Free Research Tools

  • Basketball-Reference: Game logs, splits, advanced stats
  • Pro-Football-Reference: NFL player and team data
  • FanGraphs: MLB advanced statistics
  • Natural Stat Trick: NHL analytics
  • Team websites: Injury reports, lineup confirmations

Sport-Specific Prop Tips

NFL Props

  • Receiving props: Target share is more predictive than past receptions
  • Rushing props: Consider game script - will they be running or passing?
  • Anytime TD scorer: Red zone targets/carries matter most
  • Passing yards: Consider opponent pass defense ranking AND if team will be behind (garbage time passing)

NBA Props

  • Points props: Usage rate + defensive matchup = edge
  • Rebounds: Look at opponent's offensive rebound rate and pace
  • Assists: Consider which teammates are playing (more scorers = more assist opportunities)
  • 3-pointers made: High variance. Often better to take unders.

MLB Props

  • Strikeouts: Pitcher K rate + opponent K rate = projection
  • Hits/total bases: Consider platoon splits (L/R matchups)
  • Runs: Ballpark factors matter significantly
  • Pitcher outs: Pitch count trends and bullpen usage

NHL Props

  • Shots on goal: More consistent than goals/points
  • Goalie saves: Consider opponent's shot volume
  • Points: Power play time affects point totals significantly

Common Prop Betting Mistakes

Mistake #1: Only Looking at Averages

Season averages hide important variance. A player averaging 20 points might have games ranging from 8 to 35. Understanding distribution matters.

Fix: Look at median performance and recent trends, not just means.

Mistake #2: Ignoring Correlation in SGPs

Same Game Parlays are popular but often mispriced. If you bet a QB Over passing yards AND a WR Over receiving yards, those outcomes are correlated - both require the passing game to work.

Fix: Use correlation to your advantage. Combine props that move together.

Mistake #3: Chasing Plus Money

Long-shot props like "First TD Scorer" or "Exact Score" look attractive but carry massive house edges. The juice on these props is often 20-40%.

Fix: Stick to -110 to +150 props where edges are more achievable.

Mistake #4: Not Checking Injury Reports

Props are set based on expected lineups. A teammate's injury can significantly impact a player's prop lines, but books don't always adjust quickly.

Fix: Check injury reports close to game time. Early prop bets can be outdated.

Final Thoughts

Prop betting rewards research and specialization. Instead of trying to beat the spread on every game, focus on specific players and situations where you can develop real expertise.

The most successful prop bettors:

  1. Specialize in specific sports/leagues
  2. Build databases of historical prop results
  3. Line shop across multiple books
  4. Focus on matchups, not just averages
  5. Track their results to identify strengths

Props are the sharpest edge in sports betting for those willing to put in the work.
