Parlay Betting Strategy Guide

The math says parlays are sucker bets. But used strategically, they can be powerful tools. Here's how to parlay smarter.

The Truth About Parlays

Let's be direct: parlays have a higher house edge than straight bets. The sportsbooks love them because most bettors use them wrong. But that doesn't mean you should never bet parlays.

The key is understanding when parlays make sense and how to structure them for maximum edge.

Quick Math: A 2-leg parlay at -110/-110 pays +264. But the true odds of hitting both (assuming 50% each) is +300. That's where the house edge lives.

Parlay Math You Need to Know

Legs Typical Payout True Odds (50%) House Edge
2 +264 +300 ~9%
3 +596 +700 ~13%
4 +1228 +1500 ~15%
5 +2435 +3100 ~18%
6+ Varies Varies ~20%+

As you can see, the house edge increases with every leg. This is why 10-leg parlays are essentially lottery tickets.

When Parlays Actually Make Sense

1. Correlated Parlays

This is the #1 way to get value from parlays. Correlated parlays combine bets that are more likely to win together than the math suggests.

Example: NFL Correlation Play

Game Total Under + Underdog ML

If a game goes under, it's often because defenses dominated. This favors underdogs who can win ugly 17-14 games. These outcomes are correlated.

Example: NBA Correlation Play

Team Total Over + Team to Win

If the Celtics score 125 points, they're probably winning. These bets move together.

Pro Tip: Most sportsbooks have caught onto correlated parlays and block the most obvious ones. You need to find correlations they haven't blocked yet.

2. Same Game Parlays (SGPs)

SGPs let you combine correlated bets the books would normally block. The catch: the odds are adjusted down. But sometimes the correlation is worth more than the adjustment.

  • Good SGP: Star player points over + team to win (if he scores, they probably win)
  • Bad SGP: Random props that have no relationship

3. Arbitrage/Hedge Situations

Sometimes parlay odds create arbitrage opportunities against other books. This requires multiple accounts and quick action, but it's mathematically profitable.

4. Bonus Abuse

Many sportsbook promos specifically boost parlay odds. "Parlay insurance" and "odds boosts" can flip the math in your favor.

Parlay Strategies That Work

The 2-Leg Max Rule

Limit yourself to 2-leg parlays. The house edge is lowest here (9% vs 20%+ for 5+ legs). Two strong plays combined beats five questionable ones.

The Correlated Pairs Strategy

Only parlay bets that have natural correlation. Under + Underdog. Over + Favorite. First Half + Full Game. These pairs win together more than the math expects.

The Bonus Hunter Strategy

Only bet parlays when there's a promotion involved. Parlay insurance, profit boosts, and odds boosts can make parlays +EV.

Parlay Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Adding Legs for Fun
"It's already a 3-leg parlay, might as well add a 4th!" This thinking destroys bankrolls. Every leg you add increases house edge.
  1. Never parlay heavy favorites. -300 favorites add almost nothing to your payout but cut your win probability significantly.
  2. Don't chase with parlays. Down for the day? A 5-leg parlay won't save you.
  3. Avoid "safe" legs. There's no such thing. That -500 favorite can lose, and now your whole parlay is dead.
  4. Don't bet parlays you can't explain. If you can't articulate why these bets are correlated, you're gambling.

Parlay Sizing

Because parlays have higher variance, size them smaller than straight bets:

  • 2-leg parlay: 0.5-1 unit
  • 3-leg parlay: 0.25-0.5 units
  • 4+ leg parlay: 0.1-0.25 units (lottery ticket territory)
The 1% Rule: Never risk more than 1% of your bankroll on any single parlay. They lose more often than they win.

The Bottom Line

Parlays aren't inherently bad - they're just misused. Follow these rules:

  1. Stick to 2-leg parlays maximum
  2. Only parlay correlated bets
  3. Take advantage of promotions
  4. Size your bets appropriately (small)
  5. Never add legs "for fun"

Do this, and parlays become a strategic tool rather than a bankroll killer.

Remember: The goal isn't to hit a 10-leg parlay once. It's to make profitable decisions consistently over time. Most of your action should still be straight bets.
