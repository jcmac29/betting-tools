Key Numbers in Football

Football is the only major sport where specific numbers matter significantly. Because of the scoring system (6 for TD, 3 for FG, 1-2 for PAT/2pt), certain margins of victory occur much more frequently than others.

The Most Important Key Numbers

These numbers represent the most common margins of victory in NFL history:

3 7 6 10 14 4 1

  • 3: Most common margin (~15% of games). Field goal difference.
  • 7: Second most common (~9% of games). One touchdown difference.
  • 6: TD without extra point, or two field goals.
  • 10: TD + FG difference.
  • 14: Two touchdown difference.

How to Use Key Numbers

When shopping for lines, a half-point around a key number is worth paying extra for. Getting Chiefs -6.5 instead of -7 is massive - it's the difference between pushing and losing on one of the most common margins.

Rule: Buy off of 3 and 7 when possible. Sell onto them when you're on the other side.

Betting Spreads Like a Sharp

The point spread is where most of the sharp money goes in NFL betting. Here's how professionals approach it.

1. Line Movement Analysis

Watch how lines move from open to close:

  • Reverse line movement: Line moves opposite to public betting = sharp action
  • Steam moves: Sudden, coordinated line movement across multiple books = respected money
  • Opening line value: Beating the closing line is the ultimate measure of sharp betting

2. Home Field Advantage

NFL home field advantage has decreased over the years. Current data suggests:

  • Average home field advantage: ~2.5 points (down from 3+ historically)
  • Dome teams traveling outdoors in cold weather: bigger disadvantage
  • West coast teams playing 1pm ET games: travel/time zone disadvantage
  • Altitude (Denver): still a real factor

3. Rest Advantages

Rest matters more in football than any other sport:

Situation Historical Edge
Coming off bye vs. not ~2-3 points
Extra rest (Thursday game previous week) ~1.5-2 points
Short rest (Thursday game this week) Disadvantage ~1-2 points
Cross-country travel ~0.5-1 point

Totals (Over/Under) Strategy

NFL totals are where many sharps find consistent value. The public tends to bet overs, creating value on unders in many spots.

Factors That Move Totals

  • Weather: Wind over 15mph significantly reduces scoring. Rain matters less than wind.
  • Pace: Fast-paced offenses = more plays = more scoring opportunities
  • Defensive matchups: Elite pass rush reduces scoring more than coverage
  • Game script expectations: Blowouts often go under (running clock)

Weather Betting Tip

Check weather forecasts closer to game time. Lines are set early in the week. If wind picks up significantly, unders become more valuable. The market often underreacts to weather.

First Half vs. Full Game Totals

NFL first halves tend to be lower scoring than second halves because:

  • Teams are still feeling out the opponent
  • Play-calling is more conservative early
  • Clock management in 4th quarter leads to quick scores

This creates potential value on first half unders in many games.

NFL Player Props

Player props are where sportsbooks are most vulnerable. They can't set sharp lines on every player for every game.

Passing Yards Props

Key factors:

  • Game script: Will the team be playing from behind? Trailing teams throw more.
  • Opponent pass defense ranking: DVOA or EPA allowed per dropback
  • Weather: Wind kills passing volume more than efficiency
  • Vegas total: Higher totals correlate with more passing yards

Rushing Props

Key factors:

  • Game script: Leading teams run more to kill clock
  • Opponent run defense: Yards before contact allowed
  • Offensive line health: Check injury reports
  • Snap share/touch share: More predictive than per-game averages

Receiving Props

Key factors:

  • Target share: More important than past receptions
  • Coverage matchup: Who's covering this receiver?
  • Route participation: % of team routes run
  • Red zone targets: Important for TD scorer props

Live Betting NFL

NFL live betting offers significant value because oddsmakers must react quickly to game events, and they often overreact.

Best Live Betting Opportunities

  1. After early scores: First touchdown often moves lines 3+ points. If it was a fluke (pick-six, special teams), fade the movement.
  2. Halftime: Good teams make adjustments. If a favorite is down 10, they're often getting too many points live.
  3. After turnovers: Market overreacts to turnovers. They're high variance and often don't continue.
  4. Weather changes: If wind picks up mid-game, live totals may not adjust quickly enough.

Live Betting Warning

The juice on NFL live bets is often higher (-115 to -120 instead of -110). Factor this into your value calculations. You need a bigger edge to overcome higher vig.

Common NFL Betting Mistakes

Mistake #1: Overvaluing Last Week

The public has a short memory. They overweight the most recent game. A team that lost 35-3 last week isn't suddenly terrible. Regression to the mean is real.

Fix: Look at season-long metrics, not just last week's box score.

Mistake #2: Ignoring Divisional Games

Divisional games are more unpredictable. Teams know each other well, and underdogs cover at higher rates in these matchups.

Fix: Be cautious laying big numbers in divisional games.

Mistake #3: Betting Based on Injuries Too Late

By the time injury news is public, it's priced in. The market moves fast.

Fix: Either be first on injury news or don't chase moves after announcements.

Mistake #4: Parlaying Favorites

Parlaying NFL favorites feels safe but it's a losing strategy long-term. One upset kills the whole ticket, and upsets happen weekly in the NFL.

Fix: If you parlay, use correlated props or keep it to 2-3 legs max.

Mistake #5: Not Shopping Lines

NFL spreads vary by 0.5-1 point between books regularly. Over a season, this difference is massive.

Fix: Have accounts at multiple books. Shop every bet.

Final Thoughts

Profitable NFL betting requires patience, discipline, and a systematic approach. The keys to success:

  1. Understand and respect key numbers
  2. Track line movement and know when sharp money is acting
  3. Factor in rest, weather, and travel
  4. Find edges in player props where books are soft
  5. Shop lines religiously
  6. Manage your bankroll - NFL has high variance

Football betting rewards research and preparation. Put in the work, and the edge is there to be found.
