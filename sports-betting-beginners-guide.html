Understanding Sports Betting Odds

Odds are the foundation of sports betting. They tell you two things: how likely something is to happen and how much you'll win if it does.

American Odds Explained

In the United States, most sportsbooks display American odds (also called moneyline odds). These appear as either positive (+) or negative (-) numbers.

Negative Odds (-)

Negative odds indicate the favorite. The number tells you how much you need to bet to win $100.

Example: -150 odds
Bet $150 to win $100
Total return: $250 ($150 original bet + $100 profit)

Positive Odds (+)

Positive odds indicate the underdog. The number tells you how much you'll win on a $100 bet.

Example: +200 odds
Bet $100 to win $200
Total return: $300 ($100 original bet + $200 profit)

Calculating Payouts

For negative odds: (Bet Amount / Odds) x 100 = Profit

For positive odds: (Bet Amount x Odds) / 100 = Profit

Examples:

  • $50 bet at -200: ($50 / 200) x 100 = $25 profit
  • $50 bet at +150: ($50 x 150) / 100 = $75 profit

What is "Juice" or "Vig"?

The juice (also called vigorish or vig) is how sportsbooks make money. It's built into the odds. A perfectly even bet would be +100 on both sides. But sportsbooks typically offer -110 on both sides of a point spread.

Pro Tip: The lower the juice, the better the value for bettors. Standard juice is -110, but some sportsbooks offer -105 on certain bets.

Types of Sports Bets

Moneyline Bets

The simplest bet type: pick who wins. No point spread, no complications.

Example: NFL Moneyline
Kansas City Chiefs: -180
Denver Broncos: +155

If you bet on the Chiefs at -180, you need Kansas City to win the game. Your $180 bet returns $280 ($100 profit + $180 stake).

Best for: Games where you have a strong opinion on the winner but uncertainty about the margin.

Point Spread Bets

Point spreads level the playing field between favorites and underdogs.

Example: NBA Point Spread
Boston Celtics: -6.5 (-110)
Miami Heat: +6.5 (-110)

Betting Celtics -6.5 means Boston must win by 7 or more points for your bet to win. Betting Heat +6.5 means Miami must lose by fewer than 7 points (or win outright) for your bet to cash.

Totals (Over/Under)

Totals bets don't care who wins. You're betting on the combined score of both teams.

Example: MLB Total
Over 8.5 runs: -110
Under 8.5 runs: -110

If the final score is 5-4 (9 total runs), the Over wins. If it's 4-3 (7 total runs), the Under wins.

Parlays

A parlay combines multiple bets into one. All selections must win for the parlay to pay.

Parlay Legs Approximate Odds
2+260
3+600
4+1228
5+2435
Warning: Parlays favor the sportsbook. The increased payout doesn't fully compensate for the decreased win probability. Use them sparingly for entertainment, not as a primary strategy.

Prop Bets

Proposition bets focus on specific outcomes within a game, often involving individual players.

Example Props:

  • Patrick Mahomes Over 275.5 passing yards (-115)
  • LeBron James to score first basket (+550)
  • Total strikeouts by pitcher Over 6.5 (-120)

Futures

Futures bets are placed on events that will be decided later, like championship winners or season awards.

Live Betting

Live betting (or in-play betting) allows you to bet while the game is happening. Odds update continuously based on the game's progress.

Essential Strategies for New Bettors

Start with What You Know

Your first edge as a bettor is knowledge. If you've watched football your whole life, you understand things casual observers don't. Bet on sports you genuinely follow.

Shop for the Best Lines

Different sportsbooks offer different odds. Having accounts at multiple sportsbooks is essential for serious bettors.

Track Your Bets

Keeping records helps you understand your betting patterns and identify strengths and weaknesses. Track date, sport, bet type, odds, stake, result, and profit/loss.

Avoid Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Betting with your heart: Don't let fandom cloud judgment
  • Chasing losses: Stick to your plan after losing
  • Ignoring bankroll management: See our complete bankroll guide
  • Overvaluing favorites: Public bettors love favorites, creating value on underdogs
  • Parlays as primary strategy: The house edge is significant

Responsible Gambling

Setting Limits

Most sportsbooks allow you to set deposit limits, bet limits, loss limits, and session time limits. Set these proactively.

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

Seek help if you:

  • Bet more than you can afford to lose
  • Chase losses with bigger bets
  • Lie about your betting activity
  • Neglect responsibilities due to gambling
  • Feel anxious or irritable when not betting

Resources for Help

  • National Problem Gambling Helpline: 1-800-522-4700
  • Gamblers Anonymous: gamblersanonymous.org
  • National Council on Problem Gambling: ncpgambling.org

Frequently Asked Questions

Is sports betting legal?

Sports betting is legal in over 30 US states as of 2026. Legality depends on your physical location when placing bets, not your state of residence.

How old do I need to be?

You must be 21+ to bet at online sportsbooks in most states.

Do I have to pay taxes on winnings?

Yes, gambling winnings are taxable income. Sportsbooks issue a W-2G for winnings over $600 (and 300x the wager).

What's the best sport to bet on?

Bet on sports you know best. NFL and NBA have the most betting options and analysis available.

How much should I bet?

Never bet more than you can afford to lose. A common guideline is limiting bets to 1-5% of your total bankroll.
